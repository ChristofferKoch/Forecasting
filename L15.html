ii. Nelson Combination Method

Assumption:

  • Both methods produce unbiased forecasts. That is

\[\mathbb{E}(e_{t}^{(1)}) = \mathbb{E}(e_{t}^{(2)}) = 0 \]

The estimation equation for deriging the Nelson combination (ensemble) weights is

\[ \hat{y}_{t}^{(N)}=\hat{w}_{t}^{(1)}+(1-\hat{w})f_{t}^{(2)}\] or equivalently,

\[ \hat{y}_{t}^{(N)}=\hat{w}_{t}^{(1)}+(1-\hat{w})f_{t}^{(2)}\]

iii. Granger-Ramanathan Combination Method

Assumption:

  • One or more methods produce biased forecasts. That is, for at least one fot the methdos,

\[\mathbb{E}(e_{t}^{(i)})\neq 0 \] The estimating equation for deriving the Granger-Ramanathan combination (ensemble) weights are

\[ \hat{y}_{t}^{(GR)}=\hat{w}_{0}+\hat{w}_{1}^{(1)}+\hat{w}_w f_{t}^{(2)} + \varepsilon_t\]

\[ \hat{y}_{t}^{(GR)}=\hat{w}_{0}+\hat{w}_{2}^{(1)}+\hat{w}_2 f_{t}^{(2)}\]
